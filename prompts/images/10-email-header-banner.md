# Email Header Banner — Branded

**Save as:** `assets/images/email-header.png`
**Dimensions:** 600x120
**Use:** Header image for ALL transactional emails: magic link login, weekly digest, beta confirmation, alert notifications. Must be reusable — no message-specific content.

---

Create a high-quality, ultra-sharp, crisp image at maximum resolution. Every edge must be clean and well-defined. No blur, no softness, no compression artifacts. Pixel-perfect rendering.

Generate an image with the following specifications. Follow every detail precisely.

**Subject:** A branded email header banner for "Sendvery" email communications. A smooth gradient background with a subtle geometric pattern overlay. The left third of the image should be relatively empty and dark — that's where a white "Sendvery" text logo will be overlaid in code later. The right side has subtle decorative geometric elements.

**Exact color palette — use these hex values precisely:**
- Left side: deep teal #0F766E
- Right side: transitions to deep indigo #4338CA
- The gradient flows smoothly from left to right
- Pattern overlay: slightly lighter version of the background colors, at ~10% opacity
- Decorative elements: white #FFFFFF at very low opacity (5-10%)

**Composition:**
- Exactly 600x120 pixels (standard email header dimensions)
- Horizontal gradient from teal (left) to indigo (right)
- Very subtle hexagonal or geometric grid pattern overlaid on the gradient
- Small geometric shapes (tiny hexagons, dots, thin lines) on the right side only
- Left third is clean gradient with no decorative elements — reserved for logo text overlay

**Style rules:**
- Professional and clean — this appears in every email
- Subtle and understated — not attention-grabbing, just branded
- The pattern should be barely visible — it adds texture without distraction
- Must look good in all email clients (simple shapes, no effects that email clients might break)
- No text — the "Sendvery" name will be added via HTML text overlay
- No photos, no illustrations, no icons

**What to avoid:**
- Bright or saturated colors
- Busy patterns or heavy decoration
- Sharp edges or harsh transitions
- Any text, logos, or recognizable shapes
- Complex effects that won't render in email clients
- Gradients that look banded (must be smooth)
- Blurry, soft, or out-of-focus rendering — every edge and line must be razor-sharp and crisp
