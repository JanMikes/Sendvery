# 404 Page Illustration

**Save as:** `assets/images/404.png`
**Dimensions:** 500x500
**Use:** Custom 404 error page. Displayed above "Page not found" heading.

---

Create a high-quality, ultra-sharp, crisp image at maximum resolution. Every edge must be clean and well-defined. No blur, no softness, no compression artifacts. Pixel-perfect rendering.

Generate an image with the following specifications. Follow every detail precisely.

**Subject:** A playful but professional illustration for a 404 "page not found" error page. A stylized email envelope character appears lost — it has minimal facial features (just two simple dots for eyes, giving a slightly confused expression). The envelope floats in a void with a few scattered geometric shapes around it (fragments of a broken path or scattered breadcrumbs). A faint compass or directional arrow nearby spins aimlessly, suggesting being lost.

**Exact color palette — use these hex values precisely:**
- Background: pure white #FFFFFF
- Envelope: teal #0F766E outline, very light teal #CCFBF1 fill
- Eyes on envelope: dark slate #334155 (two simple dots)
- Scattered geometric fragments: indigo #4338CA at 30% opacity
- Compass/arrow: amber #F59E0B
- Shadows: very subtle, light gray

**Composition:**
- Exactly 500x500 pixels, square
- Envelope character centered, slightly tilted (5-10° rotation suggests confusion)
- 4-6 small geometric fragments scattered around (small triangles, dots, dashes)
- Compass or directional arrow small, to one side
- Generous whitespace (at least 20% padding)

**Style rules:**
- Flat geometric with subtle depth — matches the empty state illustration style
- Light-hearted and slightly whimsical, but NOT childish or cartoonish
- The envelope's "face" is minimal — literally just two dots for eyes. No mouth, no eyebrows, no other features
- The "lost" feeling comes from the tilt, scattered fragments, and spinning compass — not from a sad expression
- Professional enough for a SaaS product — a developer should smile, not cringe
- Medium line weights (2-3px feel)

**What to avoid:**
- Overly cute or cartoonish style
- Dark or moody atmosphere
- Complex facial expressions — keep it to two dots only
- Broken screens, error symbols, or negative tech imagery
- Any text (no "404", no "Not Found") — text will be in HTML
- 3D rendering
- Blurry, soft, or out-of-focus rendering — every edge and line must be razor-sharp and crisp
