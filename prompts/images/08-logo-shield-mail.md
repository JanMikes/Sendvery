# Logo Concept — Shield + Mail

**Save as:** `assets/images/logo-concept-2.png`
**Dimensions:** square
**Use:** Logo concept exploration ONLY. The final logo must be recreated as a vector (SVG). This is for direction, not production use.

---

Create a high-quality, ultra-sharp, crisp image at maximum resolution. Every edge must be clean and well-defined. No blur, no softness, no compression artifacts. Pixel-perfect rendering.

Generate an image with the following specifications. Follow every detail precisely.

**Subject:** A minimal logo icon concept that integrates a shield shape with an email/mail element. The shield provides the outer form and the mail element is subtly embedded within it. This symbolizes "email protection and security." Show 4 variations of this concept in a 2x2 grid.

**Exact colors:**
- Icon: single solid color, deep teal #0F766E
- Background: pure white #FFFFFF

**Composition:**
- 4 distinct variations arranged in a 2x2 grid
- Variation 1: shield outline with a small envelope shape cut out of the center (negative space)
- Variation 2: shield where the bottom V-point transforms into an envelope flap
- Variation 3: envelope shape inside a shield outline — the envelope is inscribed within
- Variation 4: the shield and envelope share edges — one continuous outline forms both shapes

**Style rules:**
- Flat vector style — no gradients, no 3D, no shadows, no textures
- Single color only (teal on white)
- Ultra-minimal — must be recognizable at 16x16 pixels (favicon size)
- Clean geometric lines — sharp, precise, professional
- The icon should work as: favicon, navbar logo, app icon
- Each variation should be distinctly different from the others
- Think Linear, Notion, Stripe logomark quality — geometric, iconic, timeless

**What to avoid:**
- Complex or detailed shapes
- Multiple colors or gradients
- Rounded/bubbly/playful style
- Realistic rendering of either shield or envelope
- Any text or letters
- Thick or inconsistent line weights
- Blurry, soft, or out-of-focus rendering — every edge and line must be razor-sharp and crisp
