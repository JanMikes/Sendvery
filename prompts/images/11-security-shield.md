**Subject:** A 3D render of a large translucent glass shield in the center, protecting a cluster of small floating email envelope icons behind it. The shield has subtle hexagonal patterns etched on its surface. On the protected side (right), emails glow clean teal — they're verified and safe. On the attacking side (left), a few red-tinted envelope shapes are being deflected off the shield's surface, bouncing away.

**Exact color palette — use these hex values precisely:**
- Background: pure dark slate #0F172A
- Shield: translucent teal #0F766E with glass morphism effect (frosted glass, visible edges)
- Hexagonal etchings on shield: lighter teal at ~20% opacity
- Protected emails (right): teal #0F766E glow
- Deflected emails (left): red #EF4444, slightly transparent, motion blur
- Shield edge glow: indigo #4338CA
- Ambient light: soft white from above

**Composition:**
- Exactly 1200x600 pixels, wide format
- Shield centered, angled slightly (30° tilt) for dramatic perspective
- 3-4 clean teal emails floating safely behind the shield (right side)
- 2-3 red emails bouncing off the shield's left surface with motion trails
- Soft particle effects around the impact points
- Dark background with very subtle grid far in the background

**Style rules:**
- Premium 3D product visualization — not illustration, not photorealism, but polished 3D render
- Glass morphism on the shield — translucent, refractive, frosted edges
- Soft, cinematic lighting — dramatic but not dark
- The shield is the hero — large, prominent, confidence-inspiring
- Motion blur on the deflected (red) emails adds dynamism
- Clean negative space around the edges
- No text, no UI, no labels

**What to avoid:**
- Flat or 2D style
- Cartoonish rendering
- Too many objects — keep it simple (1 shield, 3-4 good emails, 2-3 bad emails)
- Bright or neon glow effects
- Any text or readable characters
- A shield that looks like a medieval/fantasy shield — this is geometric and modern
- Blurry, soft, or out-of-focus rendering — every edge and line must be razor-sharp and crisp
